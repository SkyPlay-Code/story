The beginning...

The sky has been bruised purple all afternoon, indicating a storm. The final bell rings, corridor floods with the usual chaos. But today is different...

As you step outside, the first heavy drops of rain begin to fall on your hand; and within seconds, it’s not a shower, but a furious, scary downpour. The kind that turns the world into a gray, blurry watercolor painting.

You put your bag over your head, yet knowing the fact that you are going to be soaked. You don't have an umbrella. You never do when it's important.

You take a step out from under the school's shed, ready to go in the cold...

...but the rain never hits you.

A shadow has fallen over you. You look up. A huge, black umbrella is now a silent shield above your head. You don't have to turn to know who it is.

The air is suddenly electric, you feel the goosebumps, smelling of rain and something else... something that one could only know after hundreds of glances, the red string of fate.

You turn slowly. It is him.

Mr. Sun-kissed.

The moment you saw him, it felt like the world around you slowed down. He doesn't say a word. He gives a gentle smile. His eyes... those deep, empathetic Pisces eyes...

They are fixed on you, but you know they are not soft right now. They are dark. Intense.

He just stands there holding the umbrella over both of you, a space where only you & him exists.

"Come on"

he says gently but his voice a low rumble under the sound of rain.

You start walking. The two of you squeezed under the umbrella, so close that your shoulder brush with him every step. You can feel the warmth radiating from him.

The world outside is a blur of gray & flashing headlights, but inside your little space, everything is quiet except your own heart hammering in chest.

You risk a glance up at him. He isn't looking ahead. He is looking at you. He is watching the way the raindrops clings to your hair tips like tiny diamonds. He's watching the way you shiver... he knows it's not just cold.

You finally reach the bus stop. It was empty. This is it.

This is where the moment ends... or so you thought.

You start to pull away to thank him, to escape back where you were.

He doesn't let you.

He takes one step closer, closing the umbrella with sharp THUMP noise, & suddenly the sound of rain is all around you again. He has trapped you against the wall of the bus stop, one hand braced against the glass behind your head.

He is so close, you can feel his breath, see the real storm swirling in his eyes.

It happened so suddenly that you froze there.

"You drive me crazy, you know that?"

he whispers, and it sounds like an accusation. "The way laugh with other boys. The way you act like you don't feel me watching you."

You can barely breath. You couldn't process what to say. His gaze drops to your lips. You see the muscle in his jaw, biting & clenching. He's fighting himself and he is looking desperate.

"I can't do this anymore"

he says, more to himself than to you.

He grabs your face with his other hand, leans & in no time he is kissing you. It's not soft. It is not gentle. It's a collision between the lips who were trying to get this exact feel.

It's everything he hadn't said in months, all stolen glances, all silent jealousy, all the protective energy, crash into you at once.

It tastes like... you know, your lips are touching his, its going, he's not stopping. His soft fluffy lips is onto you right now.

One of his hands come up to cradle the back of your neck; his fingers tangling in your hairs, holding you in place like he is afraid you will disappear.

He is not just kissing you, he is claiming you.

He is stealing breath from your lungs & giving his. The intense warmth that's coming from the kiss is... undeniable.

He pulls back, just an inch, his forehead resting against yours. You are both breathing hard, the rain covering his skin.

You are holding his collar. Eyes still closed.

Then, the Pisces in him resurfaces. The possessiveness softens just for a second, into that empathy you fell in love with. His thumb gently brushes your lower lip, which is still wet from his kiss.

"Are you cold?"

he asks, his voice now softer, like worried murmur.

Before you answer, he gives you his coat. From the distance your drive can be seen. Before you go...

...he leans in and give you one last, soft kiss. A promise.

"I will see you tomorrow"

he says & whispers "You are mine now."

And then he is gone, disappearing back into the rain, leaving you drenched, breathless & marked by him.

Leaving you to think if tomorrow will be normal. But finally he has claimed you.

The next day...

Your heart is a hummingbird trapped in your ribs. You try to focus on the teacher's lecture, but it’s just white noise. Every nerve in your body is screamingly aware of him.

The way his pen scratches against the paper. The quiet sound of his breathing. The ghost of his lips still burned onto yours from the night before.

That smile he gave you... it was worse than an intense stare. It was a secret, a quiet confirmation between two people in a room full of strangers. It said, "I remember everything. And I know you do too."

You force yourself to look at the board, your cheeks burning. You can't handle this. The silence is too loud.

Just as you think you are going to explode from the tension, something appears in your peripheral vision. A small, folded piece of paper, appearing on the edge of your desk as if by magic.

Your fingers tremble as you pick it up. You unfold it under your desk. The handwriting is rushed, but you'd recognize it anywhere:

"Did you sleep well? Or were you up all night thinking about the rain?"

Your breath catches. "The rain." That’s what he’s calling it. A secret code for the storm that happened between you.

A few minutes later, you feel a presence beside you. You don't have to look. He moved. He didn't ask. He just picked up his chair and his books, and now he is sitting right next to you.

The air between you is thick, electric. You are both staring straight ahead, pretending to listen to the lesson, but this is a different kind of class now.

Then you feel it.

The subtle, deliberate pressure of his knee brushing against yours under the desk. It’s not an accident. He lets it rest there. It’s a quiet, possessive touch in a room where everyone can see you, but no one knows what is really happening.

It's a current of electricity running straight from his leg to yours. He is marking his territory. He is reminding you who you belong to.

You can't move. You can't breathe.

He leans in, his head close to yours, as if he is about to ask a question about the lesson. The scent of rain and him fills your senses again. His voice is a low, dangerous whisper, meant only for you.

"Stay after class. Meet me at the bus stop. We need to talk."

Just as he pulls away, the bell rings, sharp and loud, shattering the moment. He stands up, gives you one last, quick, unreadable glance, and disappears into the crowd.

Leaving you to wonder... what happens when the storm returns?

Later at the bus stop...
"You came."

He breathes out. It’s not a statement; it’s a relief. The tension in his shoulders drops just an inch.

"I told myself you wouldn't," he says quietly, looking at his shoes then back at you. "That I scared you off."

You shake your head, wanting to speak, but your voice is stuck in your throat. He takes a step closer. The distance between you is gone. The air isn't cold anymore; it’s warm with the heat radiating off him.

He doesn't trap you against the wall this time. He doesn't have to. You are frozen to the spot by his gaze alone.

He reaches out, his hand hovering for a second before his fingers brush against your cheek. It’s a feather-light touch, completely opposite to the desperate grip from yesterday.

His thumb traces the line of your jaw, his eyes following the movement as if he’s memorizing you.

"I couldn't sleep."

He admits, his voice rough. "Every time I closed my eyes, I was back here. With you. Tasting the rain on your skin."

Your heart skips a beat. He leans in, his forehead resting against yours again, recreating the moment of the storm even though the sky is clear.

"I need to know."

He whispers, his eyes squeezing shut for a moment as if he's in pain. "Was yesterday just the adrenaline? Was it just the storm?"

He pulls back just enough to look into your eyes, searching deep into your soul. The vulnerability in his face breaks your heart and makes it race at the same time.

"Because for me..." He pauses, his jaw clenching again and holding you more tightly.

"For me, it was the only thing that has made sense in a long time. I don't want to just steal kisses in the rain. I want to be the reason you smile in class. I want to be the one you look for when you walk into a room."

He waits, his hand still cupping your face. He is the storm, but right now, he is waiting for you to decide if you want to step into it.

"Tell me to stop."

He says, his voice low and dangerous. "Tell me you didn't feel it too. And I'll walk away."

The silence stretches, heavy and thick. He’s daring you to lie. He knows you can't.

So... do you tell him to stop?

Or do you tell him the truth?

He takes your silence for rejection. The hope in his eyes fractures, replaced by a dull, aching resignation. He nods, once, a sharp, painful movement.

His hand drops from your face, and the loss of his warmth feels like ice water splashed over your skin.

"Okay... okay."

He whispers, his voice breaking. He turns to leave. He actually takes a step away, back towards the empty road. Back towards a life where you are just strangers.

Panic, sharp and sudden, pierces through your chest. The fear of him leaving is so much stronger than the fear of the storm.

"Don't."

You breathe out. It comes out as a squeak, but he freezes. You take a step forward. You don't think; you just react. You reach out and grab the fabric of his shirt, his collar, bunching it in your fist, anchoring him to you. You pull him back, hard.

He spins around, his eyes wide, confusion conflicting with hope.

"I can't," you say, your voice gaining strength. You look right into those deep, dark Pisces eyes. "I can't tell you to stop. Because I don't want you to."

The air leaves the space between you.

"Say it," he growls, stepping back into your space, his hands finding your waist. "Tell me you feel it. Tell me I’m not crazy."

"It wasn't just the rain," you confess, your heart hammering against your ribs. "It wasn't just the adrenaline. You... you are everywhere. In the quiet moments. In the noise. I look for you too."

A sound rips from his throat—half-laugh, half-groan. The restraint snaps.

He doesn't ask this time. He doesn't hesitate. He sweeps you up—literally lifts you off the ground—so that you are eye-level with him. Your legs instinctively wrap around his waist, locking you together.

"Good."

He breathes against your lips. "Because I was never going to let you go anyway."

And then he kisses you.

If yesterday was a collision, this... this is a devouring. It is raw, desperate, and terrifyingly beautiful. He kisses you like a man starving, like he is trying to breathe you in and keep you there.

His hands grip you tighter, possessing you, grounding you. You bury your hands in his hair, pulling him closer, melting into the heat of him.

There is no rain today, but you are drowning anyway. Drowning in him.

He breaks the kiss, gasping for air, but he doesn't put you down. He rests his forehead against yours, a wild, conquering grin breaking across his face. The sun-kissed boy is back, but now he is burning bright just for you.

"You're in trouble now," he whispers, his voice vibrating through your chest. "You told me the truth. Now I'm never leaving you alone."

He kisses the tip of your nose, then your cheek, lingering there.

"Let's go."

He says, shifting your weight easily in his arms.

"But where?" you ask, breathless.

He looks at you with that fierce, protective intensity that melts your knees.

"Anywhere," he says. "As long as it's just us."


Fin.
